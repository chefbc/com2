# Paint correction

Paint correction is the process of removing defects from your vehicle’s clear coat and paint—swirls, light scratches, water spots, and oxidation—so the finish looks smooth, deep, and reflective again. For Wisconsin vehicles that face road salt, sun, and wear, it’s one of the most effective ways to restore and protect your investment.

## Why it matters

Over time, washing, weather, and use leave fine scratches and haze in the clear coat. That dulls the look of the paint and makes it harder for sealants and coatings to bond and last. Paint correction levels the surface so light reflects cleanly and any protection you add—including [ceramic coating](ceramic-coating/ceramic.md)—performs at its best.

## How we do it

We assess your paint’s condition and recommend a level of correction that fits your goals and budget:

- **Light correction** — Reduces swirls and light marring; improves gloss without removing much clear coat.
- **Moderate to heavy correction** — Addresses deeper scratches and oxidation for a show-quality finish.

We use advanced techniques and products, including **NSP (Nano Surface Preparation) Paint Correction Technology**, to get the surface ready for long-term protection. Proper correction before a ceramic coating ensures the coating adheres well and lasts for years.

## When to choose paint correction

- You’re planning a [ceramic coating](ceramic-coating/ceramic.md) and want the best possible base.
- Your paint looks dull, scratched, or hazy and you want it to look new again.
- You’re selling or showing the car and want the finish to stand out.

For lighter cleanup without full correction, our [detailing packages](detailing-packages/index.md) (Silver, Gold, Platinum) include steps like clay and light polish where appropriate.
